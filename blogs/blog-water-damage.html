What to Do When Your iPhone Gets Wet

Published: March 15, 2024
Read time: 5 minutes
By: Alex Johnson

Accidents happen, and when your iPhone comes into contact with water, it's crucial to act quickly and correctly. Whether it's a spilled drink, an unexpected rain shower, or a drop in the toilet, following the right steps can mean the difference between a simple repair and permanent damage. In this guide, we'll walk you through exactly what to do when your iPhone gets wet.

Important Warning

Never attempt to charge a wet iPhone, and avoid pressing buttons or using heat sources like hair dryers, as these can cause further damage.

Immediate Emergency Steps (First 60 Seconds)

Time is critical when dealing with water damage. Follow these steps immediately:

1 Remove from Water Immediately

The longer your iPhone stays submerged, the more damage occurs. Get it out of the water as quickly as possible.

2 Power Off Immediately

Press and hold the side button and either volume button, then slide to power off. If the screen isn't responding, let the battery drain naturally.

3 Remove Case and Accessories

Take off any case, screen protector, and disconnect headphones or charging cables. Water can get trapped under these items.

4 Dry the Exterior

Gently pat your iPhone dry with a soft, lint-free cloth. Avoid rubbing, as this can push water deeper into the device.

What NOT to Do

These common mistakes can turn a salvageable situation into a permanent loss:

  • Don't use a hair dryer: The heat can damage internal components and push moisture deeper into the device.
  • Don't charge it: This can cause a short circuit and permanent damage to the logic board.
  • Don't press buttons: This can push water deeper into the device.
  • Don't use compressed air: This can force moisture into areas it hasn't reached yet.
  • Don't put it in rice: While popular, rice can leave starch particles in your device and isn't very effective.

Pro Tip

Newer iPhones (iPhone 7 and later) have water resistance ratings, but these degrade over time and don't protect against all liquids. Always assume your iPhone isn't waterproof.

Proper Drying Process

After the immediate steps, follow this drying process:

1. Remove SIM Card Tray

Use a SIM ejector tool or paperclip to remove the SIM card tray. This provides an additional opening for moisture to escape.

2. Positioning is Key

Place your iPhone at a 45-degree angle with the charging port facing down. This allows any remaining liquid to drain out naturally.

3. Use Silica Gel Packets

If available, place your iPhone in a sealed container with silica gel packets. These are much more effective than rice at absorbing moisture.

4. Wait Patiently

Allow at least 48 hours for complete drying before attempting to power on your iPhone. Rushing this process can cause further damage.

When to Seek Professional Help

Even if your iPhone appears to be working after drying, it's still important to have it checked by professionals. Water damage can cause:

  • Corrosion that develops over weeks or months
  • Battery issues that appear later
  • Speaker or microphone problems
  • Display issues that gradually worsen

Prevention Tips for the Future

While accidents happen, you can take steps to minimize the risk:

  • Use a waterproof case if you're often around water
  • Keep drinks away from your iPhone
  • Avoid using your iPhone in the bathroom
  • Consider AppleCare+ for accidental damage coverage
  • Regularly check your iPhone's water resistance (if applicable)
